# Contract Skeleton — {{BUSINESS_NAME}} services agreement

A starting-point services agreement generalized from a working owner-operator contract
(originally a media + AI consulting business — the pattern fits any services
entrepreneur: developers, consultants, creatives, agencies). **Personalize at install
from Phase 0 answers** and have the operator's attorney review it once — **the agent
drafts from this skeleton; it is not legal counsel.** Material deviations or a
client's redlines go to the operator, not the agent.

## How to use this skeleton (agent guidance — not contract text)

- **Contracting entity is {{BUSINESS_NAME}}** — confirm the exact registered legal
  name (and LLC/Inc. suffix) with the operator before the first real contract.
- **Scope = an itemized Schedule A**: a concrete named list of what's being delivered
  (apps, workflows, consulting, training, integrations, creative deliverables —
  whatever the operator's business ships), never prose. Rollover items from a prior
  term get their own labeled list.
- **Payment plans** (get the terms right in discovery before drafting — set the
  operator's defaults at install):
  - **Default: 50% on signing, 50% on completion** (before final delivery).
  - **Longer projects: equal payments** over the projected duration (e.g. 3-4 monthly
    payments) when 50/50 doesn't fit.
  - **Annual retainer:** one yearly amount split into 12 equal monthly payments
    covering the year's itemized plan (for standing clients).
  - Other structures when the operator says so — the Payment clause takes any schedule.
- **Maintenance / support retainers** (if the operator offers them): scoped to the
  deliverables that need upkeep, priced per customer, tiered (e.g. (1) bug fixes /
  security / support, (2) + minor tweaks, (3) + ongoing feature work). Listed in
  Schedule A with a monthly amount and what the tier covers.
- **IP position — confirm the operator's stance at install.** The shipped default
  (owner-operator friendly): **{{BUSINESS_NAME}} owns the work product and all
  underlying tools/frameworks/reusable components; the client gets a perpetual,
  non-exclusive, non-transferable license for INTERNAL business use that survives
  termination — but may never resell, sublicense, or redistribute. Client owns their
  own data.** Some operators instead assign IP to the client (common in agency work) —
  clause 3 is the one to swap.
- Contract signature = **executing the agreement** (unlike a proposal, whose signature
  may be move-forward-only). The signature block below reflects that.

## TERMS

**1. RESPONSIBILITIES.** Client shall supply timely access to the systems, accounts,
data, and personnel reasonably required for the work, and review/approval feedback at
the milestones identified in Schedule A. Provider shall perform the services and
deliver the work product described in Schedule A.

**2. CHANGES IN SCOPE.** If Client requests changes or additions beyond the scope
described in Schedule A, and such changes result in additional cost to Provider
(including person-hours), the additional amounts shall be agreed in writing (email
sufficient) before the changed work begins and are payable with the next scheduled
payment.

**3. OWNERSHIP & LICENSE.** Provider owns all right, title, and interest in and to the
work product, including all underlying tools, frameworks, and reusable components, and
all intellectual-property rights therein. Provider grants Client a perpetual,
non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the delivered work product for Client's
internal business purposes. This license survives termination or expiration of this
Agreement. Client may not sell, resell, sublicense, distribute, or otherwise make the
work product available to any third party. Client retains all ownership of Client's
own data.

**4. CONFIDENTIALITY.** Each party may receive confidential or sensitive information
of the other in connection with this Agreement. Each party agrees to use such
information solely to perform under this Agreement and not to disclose it to third
parties except as necessary to perform in the usual and customary manner (and then
under obligations of confidentiality), or as required by law.

**5. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR.** Provider's status under this Agreement is that of an
independent contractor, and persons engaged by Provider in performing its obligations
shall not be deemed employees of Client.

**6. PROVIDER WARRANTIES.** Provider represents and warrants that it has full right to
enter into this Agreement and perform its obligations, and will comply with all
applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations, and that it will use
reasonable efforts to obtain all licenses and consents necessary for materials and
services provided by Provider.

**7. CLIENT WARRANTIES.** Client represents and warrants that it has the authority to
enter into this Agreement and that it holds the rights to all materials, data, and
third-party accounts it supplies for the work. Client shall pay Provider per the
payment schedule above; where a payment is tied to completion, it is due upon
completion of the applicable deliverables and before final delivery.

**8. PAYMENT.** The specified terms are based on timely payment when invoiced. If
Client defers payment beyond the due date, Client may be charged, at Provider's
discretion and as additional consideration, an amount equal to the current prime rate
plus 10% (as charged by Provider's bank) on unpaid amounts until paid, compounded
monthly.

**9. INDEMNIFICATION.** Client agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Provider
and its officers, employees, agents, and licensees from and against any claims,
actions, damages, liabilities, and expenses arising out of the breach of any
obligation, warranty, or representation in this Agreement. Each party shall indemnify
the other against claims and expenses arising from uses for which the indemnifying
party lacked rights or authority. Client is responsible for any licensing or royalty
fees resulting from Client's unauthorized use of third-party software, content, or
services.

**10. TAXES.** Any sales, use, or other tax payable on the services or deliverables
(other than taxes on Provider's own purchases of materials) is the responsibility of
Client, who shall pay, defend, and hold Provider harmless from such taxes.

**11. ASSIGNMENT.** This Agreement may not be assigned by either party without the
written consent of the other.

**12. PORTFOLIO USE.** Provider may reference the project and non-confidential
outcomes for Provider's own promotional and portfolio purposes, unless Client requests
otherwise in writing.

**13. DISPUTE RESOLUTION.** Prior to and as a condition of either party's filing suit,
the parties shall in good faith attempt to resolve any dispute through mediation in
accordance with the mediation rules and practices applicable in {{STATE}}, until
settlement is reached or an impasse is declared by the mediator. The prevailing party
in any legal action shall be entitled to attorney's fees and costs in connection with
the legal proceedings.

**14. ENTIRE AGREEMENT AND MODIFICATION.** This Agreement and any Schedules attached
hereto constitute the entire agreement between Provider and Client. Any amendment must
be in writing and signed by each party.

**15. GENERAL.** Captions are for convenience only. Failure to exercise any right is
not a waiver of it. If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions
are unaffected. Provider agrees not to discriminate against any employee or applicant
on any basis protected by federal, state, or local law.

**16. GOVERNING LAW.** This Agreement shall be governed by the internal laws of the
State of {{STATE}}, without regard to conflicts-of-law principles. The parties submit
to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in {{STATE}} for
all disputes arising under this Agreement.
